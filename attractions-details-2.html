King Richard III Visitor Centre

★★★★★
4A St Martins, Leicester LE1 5DB
0116 229 4132

The King Richard III Visitor Centre is built directly over the historic site where the king was buried after his defeat at the Battle of Bosworth in 1485. Lost to history for over 500 years, his remains were dramatically rediscovered in 2012. The centre offers a captivating journey through Richard III’s life, death and remarkable rediscovery.

Leo ★★★★★

Absolutely fascinating exhibition that tells the remarkable story of the discovery of Richard III. The use of multimedia is excellent and really brings history to life.

Priya ★★★★★

A must-visit in Leicester! The exhibition is thoughtfully laid out and provides incredible insight into the painstaking process of identification. The glass floor over the actual excavation site is particularly moving.

Robert ★★★★★

Very informative and well presented. The exhibits are engaging and suitable for all ages. The story of how they identified the king's remains through DNA and other evidence is fascinating.
